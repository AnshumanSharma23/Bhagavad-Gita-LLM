He who neither rejoices, nor hates, nor grieves, nor desires, renouncing good and evil,
and who is full of devotion, is dear to Me.
COMMENTARY: He does not rejoice when he attains desirable objects nor does he grieve
when he parts with his cherished objects. Further, he does not desire the unattained.
Samah shatrau cha mitre cha tathaa maanaapamaanayoh;

18. He who is the same to foe and friend, and in honour and dishonour, who is the same in
cold and heat and in pleasure and pain, who is free from attachment,
Tulyanindaastutirmaunee santushto yena kenachit:
Aniketah sthiramatir bhaktimaan me priyo narah.
19. He to whom censure and praise are equal, who is silent, content with anything,
homeless, of a steady mind, and full of devotion—that man is dear to Me.
