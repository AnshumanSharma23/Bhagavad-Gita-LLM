All the created worlds, gods, beings, creatures
and things stand revealed as the one gigantic body of the Lord.
Arjuna further sees that the great cosmic drama is set in motion and controlled by the
all-mighty power of the Lord. His Will alone prevails in all things and actions, both good and bad.
The Lord exhorts him to fight, he being only an apparent cause of the destruction of his enemies.
Arjuna is unable to bear the pressure of the sudden expansion of consciousness and is filled
with fear. He begs the Lord to assume once more His usual form.
Krishna reiterates that this vision cannot be had through any amount of austerities, study,
sacrifices or philanthrophic acts. Supreme devotion is the only means by which one can have access
to His grand vision.
